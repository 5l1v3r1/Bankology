Spending Wisely

Spending wisely is when you budget your money so that you can have enough money for basic needs, wants, and savings. For example some people use the 50 30 20 rule. The 50 30 20 rule divides you money into three basic categories; needs, wants, savings. It is divided into 50% for needs, 30% for wants, and 20% for savings. Although this is a good starting point, it is important to note that everyone is in a different situation, so your categories and percentages are going to be different.

(section 2)

Spending wisely is a very important skill to have because when you are young, your parents do it for you. However as you grow up it is important to budget your own money so that you don't go into debt. Falling into debt is very easy if you don’t budget your money because if you don’t have a limit on how much to spend, you will probably go over how much money you have available. One thing to note is that although you should try to stay to this plan, sometimes you have to go outside of the budget, and that is okay. The most important thing is to get back into the routine of the budget, after you go outside of it.
